▶ How to Use This System — A quick reference guide for navigating the system.
Step 1
Materials Stock
Raw Materials shows everything currently on hand, including quantities and units. The materials needed to make a recipe must exist here first.
Step 2
Recipes
All Recipes shows every recipe you have created. New ones are built from existing Raw Materials. A recipe must exist before you can create a batch that uses it.
Step 3
Batches
When a batch is made, log it with Create New Batch. Pick the recipe, and it tracks what was used, then saves the batch as "Ready".
Step 4
Ship It Out
When an order ships, mark the batch as Shipped from the Batches page. It moves to the shipped log automatically.
Step 5
Manage
To edit any details about Raw Materials, Batches, or Recipes, use each section's dedicated Manage page.
Step 6
Export
Most list pages have an Export to Excel button at the top right. Click it to download a spreadsheet to your computer.
Adding a new material
  1. Go to Add Material.
  2. Fill in the name, stock amount, and unit (kg, g, bags, etc.).
  3. Set a Reorder Level — the quantity at which the system flags it as low stock.
  4. Supplier and cost are optional but useful for records.
Creating a batch
  1. Go to New Batch.
  2. Choose a recipe from your list of recipes.
  3. Enter the quantity and date, then submit.
  4. The batch is saved with status Ready.
Shipping a batch
  1. Go to Batches.
  2. Find the batch and click Ship.
  3. It moves to Shipped Batches and leaves the ready list.
  4. You can also change status in Manage Batches.
Editing or deleting a record
  1. Go to Manage Materials, Manage Batches, or Manage Recipes.
  2. Click Edit to update details.
  3. Click Delete to remove it permanently.
  4. Deletes can't be undone — double-check before confirming.
Responding to low stock
  1. Go to Low Stock Alerts.
  2. Everything listed is below its reorder threshold.
  3. Export the list to Excel if you need to share it.
  4. Once restocked, update the quantity in Manage Materials.
Exporting to Excel
  1. Go to any of: Raw Materials, Low Stock, Batches, Shipped, or All Recipes.
  2. Click the Export to Excel button at the top right.
  3. A spreadsheet downloads to your computer automatically.
Good to know & in case of emergency:
The data is safe and backed up nightly — a full copy of all company data is stored every night in case of emergency The Audit Log records every change made — useful if something looks off Reorder Level is just a threshold you set — the system doesn't automatically order anything Recipes need to exist before you can create a batch that uses them If the page looks wrong, try refreshing before assuming it's a bug In case of emergency — if data is altered beyond manual fixing, contact the admin to restore the database from a nightly backup
